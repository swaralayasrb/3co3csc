Rice is the staple food of the people of Kerala, and, traditionally, the cultivation of rice has occupied pride of place in the agrarian economy of the State. The lush green ofpaddy fields is one of the most captivating features of Kerala's landscape. Paddy cultivation in Kerala has some unique features. The state's different regions grow paddy in different ways. While the crop is cultivated two to three metres above the sea level in Kuttanad, it is grown above 1,400 metres in Wayanad district on the Western Ghats.Rice can be cultivated under a variety of climatic and soil conditions. Rice cultivation is conditioned by temperature parameters at the different phases of growth. The critical mean temperature for flowering and fertilization ranges from 16 to 200C, whereas, during ripening, the range is from 18 to 320C.
